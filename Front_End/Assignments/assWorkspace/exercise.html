A breath introduction of Rio de Janeiro

introduction

Rio de Janeiro (informally referred to as Rio)is a Brazilian municipality, capital of the homonymous state, located in the Southeast of the country. Largest international tourist destination in Brazil, in Latin America and the entire Southern Hemisphere (in 2008), the capital of Rio de Janeiro is the best-known Brazilian city abroad, functioning as a "mirror", or national "portrait", either positively or negatively. It is the second largest metropolis in Brazil (after São Paulo), the sixth largest in America and the thirty-fifth in the world. Its population estimated by the IBGE for July 1, 2021 was 6,775,561 inhabitants. It has the epithet of Cidade Maravilhosa, and those born there are called cariocas.


Classified as a metropolis, it exerts national influence, whether from the Brazilian cultural, economic or political point of view, and is one of the main economic, cultural and financial centers of the country, being internationally known for several cultural and landscape icons, such as the Sugarloaf Mountain, Corcovado Mountain with the statue of Christ the Redeemer, the beaches in the neighborhoods of Copacabana, Ipanema and Barra da Tijuca, among others; the Maracanã and Nilton Santos stadiums; the bohemian neighborhood of Lapa and its arches; the Municipal Theater of Rio de Janeiro; the forests of Tijuca and Pedra Branca; Quinta da Boa Vista; the National Library; the island of Paquetá; New Year's Eve in Copacabana; the carioca carnival; Bossa Nova and samba. Part of the city was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site on July 1, 2012.
